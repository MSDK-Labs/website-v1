The Problem

AI is making regulated decisions. Most firms cannot evidence how.

During FCA review or internal audit, firms are required to explain how a recommendation or decision was produced. In many cases, the underlying AI decision cannot be reconstructed. The input data is not preserved, the reasoning path is unavailable, and the firm cannot evidence suitability or control.

01 No audit record
AI decisions are not stored in a form that allows reconstruction of input, output, and context.
02 Black-box exposure
Firms cannot demonstrate how decisions align with regulatory obligations such as Consumer Duty.
03 Logging is insufficient
Application logs capture events, not structured decision evidence suitable for audit.
04 Regulatory timeline
EU AI Act requirements for high-risk systems introduce formal expectations for decision traceability.

How It Works

Decision capture and certification within your AI architecture.

Aegis Trace operates as a decision capture and certification layer alongside your existing AI systems. It integrates via a single API endpoint within your inference workflow.

01
Submit decision payload
Your system sends a structured payload representing the AI decision, including inputs, outputs, and relevant metadata.
02
PII handling
Defined personal data fields are removed or tokenised prior to persistence, based on configurable schema rules.
03
Cryptographic signing
Each payload is hashed and signed using HMAC-SHA256 with managed key infrastructure. The resulting certificate is independently verifiable.
04
Immutable storage
Certificates are written to an append-only, WORM-locked store with defined retention and retrieval via API or export.
